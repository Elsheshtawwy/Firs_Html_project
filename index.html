Sherlock Holmes

Sherlock Holmes is a fictional character created by the Scottish writer Arthur Conan Doyle. This character first appeared in the novel A Study in Scarlet, which was published in the annual Christmas issue of Beeton’s in 1887. This character was described as the first and only consulting detective in the world, who pursued criminals all over London. Holmes had a unique influence on popular imagination and is the most enduring character used in detective stories.

Sherlock Holmes is a brilliant creation of the writer Arthur Conan Doyle , who created a character of a genius detective who uses logic and science to solve the most complex crimes. Holmes was inspired by Doyle’s professor at the medical school, Joseph Bell, who was known for his ability to observe and deduce. Holmes became a model for the modern detective, and had a great influence on the genre of the detective novel.
